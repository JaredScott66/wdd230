The Building the chamber members use is the retired Clarksville train station. Built in 1910 it was commissioned by the Missouri-Pacific Railroad and designed in Mediterranean the style which was popular at the time.

Johnson County was established in 1833 due to active trade among the Osage indans. Later on, as the outpost grew and more people settled in the area, the county seat was moved to the blooming town of Clarksville in 1836.

Hundreds of miles of hiking trails are sprawled throughout Clarksville and Johnson County. From a casual stroll on a paved path, to rugged uphill climbs through remote woodlands, there is a trail system for all ages and abilities.

The tradition began in 1938. Today the Johnson County Peach Festival is the oldest festival in the State of Arkansas.

Every summer many cars pass through Clarksville for cruises and shows. You can find classic cars from the 1920s all the way to the current hot rods.
